Academic Projects

LLR - Lanka Land Rendting Website

Design Ideation and Creative Development
ObjectiveThe project aims to simplify the process of listing, searching, and renting land through a clean and easy-to-use interface.
Your RoleFrontend Developer, Presenter
Tools / TechnologiesReact, Node.js, Tailwind CSS, VS Code

LLR (Lanka Land Renting) is an application designed to address the issue of finding and renting out lands. People needed to visit the site personally to verify the availability of land and landowners had problems identifying suitable tenants. To overcome this problem, we have designed a website through which landowners will be able to advertise their lands along with photographs. Through our website, one would be able to find appropriate lands without visiting the sites personally.


Outcomes: The end product was a functional, easy-to-use land renting website that connects land owners with potential renters. We showed that we can develop a real-world web solution through teamwork, problem solving, and fullstack development skills.

Drive Drip

Project in Technology
ObjectiveIn order to create a web-based platform for Sri Lanka’s automobile industry to facilitate real-time visualization of 3D modifications as well as the networking of all parties involved within one central hub.
Your RoleTeam Lead and Frontend Developer
Tools / TechnologiesReact, Node.js, Three.js, Django, TailwindCSS, GitHub

DRIVEDRIP is a web application that changes the way car owners buy parts and customize cars. People can choose the desired car brand and customize the car instantly through a 3D simulation process, which functions similarly to those in video games. Our web app offers the user not only an extensive parts store but also includes AI chatbots, live chat options, and certified mechanics' services. DRIVEDRIP was developed using React.js and Three.js on the frontend and Django on the backend.


Outcomes: Delivered a working 3D vehicle customization using Three.js as part of a working MVP - Conducted research with 50+ participants to validate market demand of 100% interest in visualization features. Developed and launched a responsive web app with an AI chatbot, live chat and marketplace functionality. User feedback: Positive with appreciation for the innovative concept and user-friendly design. Effectively led and collaborated with teams through using Agile development methodology

Reflection Section
Personal reflections on my university journey
Reflection 1

DriveDrip - Group Project Experience


Experience

I was the team leader of a team of five members developing DRIVEDRIP, a spare parts platform that uses 3D technology to visualize the vehicle's spare parts. I worked on the frontend of the website using React.js and Three.js, doing market research with over 50 participants. We successfully delivered a working version of the DRIVEDRIP website that included a 3D customization feature, an artificial intelligence-based chatbot, and a marketplace feature. The entire project was developed using a collaborative approach in GitHub.

Learning

My exposure to the technologies React.js, TailwindCSS, and Three.js has been very useful since all were new to me. Besides programming, my ability in research was enhanced by surveys and interviews, which taught me that feedback from the users is necessary to justify assumptions. Leadership qualities such as teamwork, communication, and conflict resolution have also been sharpened because of being the team leader.

Challenges

The use of Three.js for the purpose of creating 3D models proved to be the most challenging aspect since I had no previous experience in 3D programming. Managing five people on one project led to conflicts during code merging on GitHub due to uneven proficiency levels. Time allocation between research work and coding remained difficult. Survey results collected at the initial stages needed further interviewing.

Solutions & Future Improvements

Three.js problems were solved via online guides and forum posts. Weekly discussions, structured GitHub development practices, and code reviews were implemented to facilitate collaboration within the team. Enhanced user feedback was gained through on-site presentations rather than using surveys.

Reflection 2

CivicResolve - Hackathon Project


Experience

As part of the team working on CivicResolve, a web-based application that enables people to submit and follow up on issues regarding the state of community infrastructure such as damaged roads, I concentrated on creating the application’s graphical design and designing its front end.

Learning

It also made me proficient at making links between graphic design and practical engineering. In addition, I was able to convert community requirements into an operational digital application and develop great skills in user-centered design and Next.js based web application architecture.

Challenges

The first challenge was to strike a balance between an elaborate data reporting system and an easy-to-use interface. Furthermore, making sure that the platform is responsive and secure during real-time location tracking was technically difficult.

Solutions & Future Improvements

By implementing a modular design approach and conducting thorough testing, we were able to address the responsiveness and security concerns. For future projects, I plan to allocate more time for planning and prototyping to mitigate such technical challenges.
